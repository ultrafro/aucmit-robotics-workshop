AUC × MIT Educational Partnership

Hands-on robot learning, taught in Cairo.

Students from MIT travel to The American University in Cairo to lead an intensive robotics workshop. Participants build, teleoperate, and train real robot arms using Hugging Face's LeRobot — running on Orange Pi 5 boards with stereo camera vision.

About the workshop

From zero to a trained robot policy in one week

This is a small, hands-on education program — not a lecture series. Working in small teams with MIT instructors, participants take a robot arm from unboxing to a learned manipulation policy trained on data they collect themselves.

01

Build & teleoperate

Assemble and calibrate leader–follower robot arms, then drive them by hand to get a feel for kinematics, control loops, and the joys of real hardware.

02

Collect demonstrations

Record teleoperated demonstrations with synchronized stereo camera streams — the dataset that your robot will learn from.

03

Train & deploy

Train imitation-learning policies with LeRobot and deploy them back onto the arm. Watch a robot do the task on its own, using a model you trained.

Curriculum

Built on LeRobot

The workshop is built around LeRobot, Hugging Face's open-source library for real-world robot learning. Everything participants use is open source and inexpensive enough to keep building with after the workshop ends.

Imitation learning

How robots learn from human demonstrations: dataset formats, action chunking, and policies like ACT — explained from first principles, then trained for real.

Robot vision

Stereo cameras give the robot depth. Participants work with dual-lens webcam modules and an Intel RealSense depth camera to understand how robots perceive a 3-D workspace.

Edge deployment

Each team's stack runs on an Orange Pi 5 single-board computer — proof that serious robot learning doesn't need a server room.

Hardware

What's on each team's bench

Every station is a complete, self-contained robot-learning rig.

SO-101 robot arms

A leader–follower pair of open-source 6-DoF arms — the standard LeRobot platform for teleoperation and imitation learning.

Orange Pi 5

An 8-core ARM single-board computer with a 6-TOPS NPU, running the full data-collection and inference stack at the edge.

Stereo webcam modules

Synchronized dual-lens USB cameras provide binocular views of the workspace for policy training.

Intel RealSense D405

A short-range depth camera for high-fidelity close-up perception at the gripper.

System diagram

How the parts become a robot

Every part on the bench plays one role. You drive the leader arm by hand; the Orange Pi mirrors your motion onto the follower arm while the cameras watch. Those recordings become the dataset a policy is trained on — and then the same loop runs without you.

During teleoperation the policy is you; after training, the same camera streams and the same serial bus carry the policy's decisions instead. Nothing about the hardware changes between learning and autonomy.

Program

A typical workshop week

Day 1

Welcome & setup — meet the MIT instructors, flash the Orange Pis, assemble and calibrate the arms.

Day 2

Teleoperation — leader–follower control, camera mounting, and stereo calibration.

Day 3

Data collection — design a manipulation task and record demonstration datasets.

Day 4

Training — imitation-learning theory, then train policies on the collected data.

Day 5

Demo day — autonomous policy rollouts, team presentations, and next steps for continuing in robotics at AUC.
